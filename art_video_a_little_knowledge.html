Downloading Videos to Your Computer
All video files posted or linked to here at Outpost Daria can be downloaded to your computer (except where noted). Not only does this conserve network bandwidth, it also allows you to view the video at your leisure, any time you please. The process of downloading a video is not difficult, and is virtually the same with any web browser (Internet Explorer, Mozilla Firefox, Opera, etc.).
  1. Place the mouse cursor over the link to the video you wish to download.
  2. Right-click on the link. When the pop-up menu appears, choose the option to save the link to your hard drive. (This option is usually called "Save Link As" or "Save Target As.")
  3. Select the directory into which the video file will be stored. Note that some browsers can be configured to always save downloaded files into a default directory, in which case you will not be asked to select a directory.
  4. Once the file is downloaded, you're done!
Note to Non-PC Users: Sorry, but I'm afraid you're on your own here. Most systems should be very similar, but hardware differences (such as the Mac's one-button mouse) will require adaptations to the procedure, and since I'm a PC user, my knowledge of the workings of other systems is very limited.
