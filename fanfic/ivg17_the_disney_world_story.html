Summary: When Daria's ancient great aunt dies, everyone is shocked to learn that Daria inherited her fortune for a very unusual reason. Helen coerces Daria into taking Quinn, and all the rest of the Lawndale gang, on a vacation to what Daria claims is "The one place which goes against everything I believe in"--Disney World. Multiple adventures ensue down in sunny Florida.


Chapters: 1 2 3 4 5 6 7



CHAPTER ONE


(Scene opens in the Morgendorffer living room. Daria and Quinn are sitting on one of the sofas. Quinn is flipping through channels on the TV while Daria is looking through a stack of books.)

QUINN: Seen that.

DARIA: Read this one.

QUINN: Seen that one too.

DARIA: Read this one.

QUINN: God, look at those shoes! This one must be really old.

DARIA: Read this one twice. (She looks at Quinn.) Much as I hate to say it, I think that summer is finally reached the boring stage.

QUINN: For real! (She sighs mournfully.) I've talked to Sandi, Stacy, and Tiffany today, Cashman's has the same old stuff it had at the beginning of the summer, and I've gone out with every guy on my list twice! (She looks at Daria.) Have you run out of things to do?

DARIA: (deadpan) Yeah. I've dated all the guys on my list three times. I have an idea. Why don't we negotiate a trade? Your remote for my library card.

QUINN: Me, read? Are you out of your mind?

DARIA: (shrugs) Have it your way if you really want to go out with Paul for a third time.

(Quinn looks pained, and glances from the remote to Daria's books. Finally she closes her eyes and holds out the remote melodramatically. Just as Daria reaches to take it, the phone rings. Quinn opens her eyes joyfully.)

QUINN: I got it!

(She jumps up and runs for the phone.)

DARIA: Damn, I was this close.

(Quinn comes back into the living room carrying the cordless and looking a little puzzled as Jake and Helen walk in.)

QUINN: Daddy? It's for you.

(Jake takes the phone.)

JAKE: Hello? (pause; he looks upset) Oh, I'm so sorry! I--(another pause) Oh. Yes, of course we will. (another pause) All right. We'll be there tomorrow. Goodbye.

(He turns off the phone and looks at Helen, upset.)

JAKE: Helen, Aunt Sarah Morgendorffer's dying!

(Helen looks puzzled.)

HELEN: Who's Aunt Sarah Morgendorffer?

JAKE: (blinks, no longer upset) I thought you knew who she was.

HELEN: Never heard of the woman.

JAKE: Neither have I.

DARIA: Isn't she the one that disowned Dad after he broke her mother's best china as a kid?

JAKE: Ohh, yeah! That Aunt Sarah. Well, anyway, she's dying and she wants us to come see her tomorrow.

HELEN: That's so nice! She must have forgiven you, Jake, and she wants us to be there when she passes away!

JAKE: She damn well better have forgiven me! That was forty years ago!

HELEN: Now, Jake, you have to be nice to her. After all, it's probably about the inheritance. You know how rich she was.

QUINN: Ooh, rich? Does that mean we'll get lots of her money?

DARIA: (scowls) Quinn, she's dying. You shouldn't be thinking about--

HELEN: I should hope so! The woman's loaded. (She puts her hand on Jake's shoulder.) And after all the emotional strain she's put on you for all these years! You deserve a little compensation.

(Daria glares even more and gets up to go to her room. Helen, Jake, and Quinn watch her leave.)

QUINN: (disgusted) What's wrong with her? (brightly) When are we leaving?

(Cut to Daria up in her room. She's talking to Jane on the phone.)

DARIA: (angrily) ...so the woman's dying, and all they can think about is what they're going to get out of it!

(Cut to split screen showing both Daria and Jane.)

JANE: That sucks. I guess that's why Mom and Dad have such strange wills. They're leaving their stuff to us kids, but they're donating most of their money to different foundations and stuff. It makes sense really. I mean, the rest of the Lanes won't have anything to do with them while they're alive, so why should they get the money when my parents are dead?

DARIA: Yeah. You think you and Trent'll do the same thing?

JANE: I will. I doubt that Trent will have any money to donate.

TRENT: (offscreen VO) I heard that!

JANE: Anyway, when are you leaving?

DARIA: Tomorrow. Aunt Sarah's certain that she'll die before the week is over, and she wants us to get there in time.

JANE: Have fun.

DARIA: Jane, I'm going to spend several days surrounded by my father's family. Do you think I'll have fun?

(Cut to the Morgendorffers in the car the next morning as they're driving to Aunt Sarah's house, about three hours away from Lawndale. Daria is morosely writing in her journal as the other three discuss the will.)

HELEN: I wish she would leave us some furniture. Some of those antiques are probably worth thousands.

JAKE: And those old books of hers! I remember looking at 'em when I was a kid. (darkly) Right before she yelled at me to get away from them before I ripped all the pages out.

QUINN: I just want money.

(Daria scowls and returns to her journal writing.)

DARIA: (writing thought VO) All my family keeps talking about is stuff. What they want to get and what it might be worth. I think I'm the only one who cares that a person is dying here, and I've never even met her before!

(She sighs and puts down her journal. Cut to the sidewalk in front of Jane's house. Jane is sitting on the grass, drawing an intricate mural on the sidewalk with chalk. Trent comes out of the house and walks over to her.)

TRENT: What's this?

JANE: This is what happens when one gets so bored, she actually misses going to school.

(She sighs and puts down her chalk. Trent turns his head sideways and squints at the picture. It is still a large, pastel abstraction. Trent shrugs and sits down on the grass beside Jane.)

TRENT: I'm bored too.

JANE: (surprised) You're never bored. Don't you guys have practice or something?

TRENT: All of the guys 'cept for me and Jesse are out of town. Besides, I just don't feel like practicing. You know.

(Jane picks up the chalk again and kneels in the grass in front of a blank stretch of sidewalk. She draws the outline of a body sprawled in the sidewalk, then adds a guitar in one hand and a microphone in the other.)

JANE: If I put up some police tape, people will think you've been murdered, and you can go around pretending to be a ghost.

TRENT: What I really want is to go somewhere, have some kind of adventure. (He perks up.) I have an idea. You, me, Jesse, and Daria could go on a road trip!

(Jane gives him a Look.)

JANE: Do you really think me and Daria want to be stuck in a car with you two for the rest of the summer? (Trent narrows his eyes.) Besides, Daria's not here.

TRENT: Oh. Uh, so is she on vacation or something?

(Jane moves to another clean spot of concrete and begins doodling some more.)

JANE: Nah. Some relative of hers is dying and she called on the Morgendorffer clan to ease her through the transition to a higher plane. (Jane stops drawing and sits back on her heels.) Daria would probably be a real comfort to a dying old lady. You know, sort of as a reminder of how depressing this life is. Wherever you go next can't possibly be this bad.

(Cut to the Morgendorffers pulling up at a huge, very beautiful old house. As they get out, Helen and Quinn exclaim over the house, Daria stares straight ahead, and Jake starts grumbling something about returning to the scene of the crime.)

HELEN: (patronizingly) Now, you need to need to be very nice to Aunt Sarah. She's a very sick lady, and we don't want to provoke her.

DARIA: (thought VO) Yeah, 'cos then she might not pay up.

JAKE: That's right, girls. Listen to your mother.

HELEN: I was talking to you, Jake.

JAKE: Oh. (glares) Hey!

(The family walks up to the door, where Jake rings the bell. A very authentic-looking maid in a black and white uniform answers the door.)

QUINN: (dreamily) A maid!

DARIA: Maybe we'll inherit her.

MAID: (with a heavy accent) You must be Jake and Helen.

HELEN: Why, yes! However did you guess?

MAID: (with attitude) You're the last to arrive. Madam is not very happy with you.

DARIA: Somehow, I'm not surprised.

MAID: Follow me.

(She turns and flounces off into the house. Hesistantly, the four Morgendorffers follow. As they walk, Jake is sulking. Helen and Quinn look around in awe at the fancy house, and Daria still stares straight ahead. The maid leads them a long flight of red-carpeted stairs and points to a door. The Morgendorffers enter the room, which turns out to be Sarah's bedroom. It is packed with other Morgendorffers. Sarah is lying in bed, propped up on pillows. She is indeed a very old lady.)

SARAH: Ah. I see you finally arrived, Jake.

(Jake grumbles something, but Helen steps up his foot and he quickly reforms.)

JAKE: Dear Aunt Sarah!

(He makes his way over to her bed, followed by Helen and the girls.)

JAKE: This is my wife Helen, and our two little angels, Quinn and Daria.

HELEN & QUINN: (gushing) Hello!

DARIA: Uh, hey.

SARAH: (ignoring the greetings) Now that everyone is here, I'd like to go ahead and have my say since the lot of you finally have to listen to me.

(Cut to closeup of Daria. She smiles a little. Cut back to Sarah.)

SARAH: Obviously, I'm getting on up there in years, and I have a feeling in my bones that I'm not long for this world. That's why I dragged the bunch of you over so I could see you one last time.

(She starts coughing uncontrollably. The Morgendorffers, except for Daria, look vaguely disgusted and glance at each other. Sarah quits coughing and continues.)

SARAH: Now I want all of you to carry on and talk and have a good old time. It does my old heart good to see you, so young and happy--well, except for you, dear sister-in-law.

(Cut to a shot of Jake's mother, who is neither young nor happy. Cut to wider shot of the room as the clan begins to mingle, somewhat awkwardly. Jake starts talking to his snooty cousin Cynthia.)

CYNTHIA: Jake, dear, how have you been?

JAKE: Oh, just great! (He laughs forcedly.)

CYNTHIA: How lovely. And your two girls are growing up so fast! My dear little Jennie is just blossoming!

(She puts her arm around her daughter, who is about Quinn's age, with wavy blond hair and an outfit the Fashion Club would kill for.)

JENNIE: Mooo-ooom, you're embarrassing me!

(She pulls away from her mother and stalks off.)

CYNTHIA: (fondly) Isn't she just lovely?

(Cut to Helen, Quinn, and several other conniving women gathered around Sarah's bed. Each is trying to outdo the other when Sarah suddenly starts coughing again. The women back away until she quits.)

HELEN: Er, well. Aunt Sarah, what do you think of our Quinn? Isn't she just getting so big?

SARAH: What about your other daughter?

(Cut to shot of Daria, sitting in a chair off by herself.)

HELEN: (dismayed offscreen VO) Oh, Daria.

(Cut back to the group at the bed.)

HELEN: Daria's always been a little... different. We do our best to support her in everything she does, but you know how kids are these days. (She laughs a little.) It's hard to keep them happy. Why, I--

(She is interrupted by the ringing of a bell. Everyone turns to see the maid standing in the doorway with a hand bell.)

MAID: Dinner is ready, ladies and gentlemen. Please follow me to the dining room.

(She turns and leaves, followed by the guests, grateful for the interruption. Daria is the last to leave, when Sarah stops her.)

SARAH: Daria. Come here for a minute.

(Daria goes over to her and sits in a chair beside the bed.)

SARAH: You seem like a sensible girl. (wryly) More than the rest of them anyhow.

DARIA: (secretly pleased) Uh, thanks.

SARAH: What did you think of my cough? Pretty convincing, no?

DARIA: You mean it was fake?

SARAH: You bet. If I was that sick, I'd insist on a hospital and not this drafty old place. It's just a ploy to see their reaction.

DARIA: (laughs) I'll have to try that sometime.

SARAH: (sighs) Yeah, you come up with a lot of good ideas when you're stuck in bed for weeks on end. Too bad it's too late now to try 'em out.

DARIA: (awkwardly) Maybe it's not. I mean maybe it's not all over...

SARAH: (gently) It'd better be. I'm tired, Daria. Real tired. I'm ready to die, dammit, and no one's going to tell me otherwise. If only it wasn't such a blasted inconvenience. Sometimes I wish I'd've gotten married and had a kid just so I wouldn't have to figure out who to leave my stuff to.

DARIA: Why didn't you ever get married?

SARAH: When I was marryin' age, I was as brassy as they came, and boys didn't like that back then.

DARIA: (wryly) They still don't.

SARAH: (laughs) I figured that if I was meant to be married, well, I'd find me a man someday who'd love me the way I was. And if I wasn't meant to be married, so be it. I guess it just wasn't meant to be. I don't regret it either. I'd rather be myself than be a perfect girl just to get some boy to like me. (She looks at Daria seriously.) I think you know what I mean.

DARIA: (thoughtfully) I think I do.

SARAH: Sorry to keep carrying on so. You can go eat if you want to.

DARIA: Actually, I'm not very hungry. I, uh, have a book if you'd like me to read to you.

SARAH: What's the book?

DARIA: It's called Brave New World. (wryly) It's about a futuristic society obsessed with sex and conformity.

SARAH: I think I'd like that.

(Cut to later that night as Daria goes into the bedroom she's sharing with Quinn and Jennie. The other two girls are sitting on the bed, talking.)

QUINN: And there are these three guys who absolutely worship me.

JENNIE: Oh, I know how that is. We have to play spin the bottle just to figure out who I'm going to go out with that night!

(Quinn's eyes narrow.)

QUINN: The other girls in the Fashion Club are always telling me how up-to-date my outfits are. I guess it's only natural since I am vice-president of the club.

JENNIE: What a coincedence! We have a little club too, but we call it the Fashion Society. I'm president, you know.

QUINN: (wails) President?!?

(Daria silently turns and leaves the room. She spots a telephone sitting on a marble-top table in the hall, so she picks up the receiver and sits down in a chair to call Jane. Split screen with her and Trent's weird duck phone as Trent picks up.)

TRENT: Hello?

(Daria looks surprised and blushes a little.)

DARIA: Uh, hi Trent. Is Jane there?

TRENT: Hey, Daria. Janey and Jess went out tonight.

DARIA: (disappointed) Oh.

(There is a long silence.)

TRENT: Uh, Jane said that you were off at some relative's house?

DARIA: Yeah, my great aunt. (She glances around and lowers her voice.) It's hell here. The only other teenager is a clone of my sister, and all the adults are worse. Aunt Sarah's the only cool person here. Too bad she's the one dying.

TRENT: I feel for you. I hate family things. So, uh, what have you been doing?

DARIA: I read to Aunt Sarah all afternoon, then spent all evening listening to everyone else talk about what they were getting once she died.

TRENT: That's just like people, isn't it?

DARIA: (smiles) Yeah. It is. So... um, what are you up to?

TRENT: I was asleep.

DARIA: Oh. Sorry. I didn't mean to wake you up.

TRENT: No, it's okay. I'd been sleeping all day anyway. You know. (He pauses.) I think Janey misses you. She keeps complaining that there's nothing to do.

DARIA: (wryly) Nice to know that I'm missed.

TRENT: Yeah, well. You know, it is weird without you here. I'm used to seeing you around with Janey, and, uh, you know... (He coughs nervously.) Say, uh, do you know the phone number there? So I can, uh, give it to Janey tomorrow.

DARIA: Yeah, it's written on the phone. Got a pen?

(Trent looks around at the piles of junk in his room.)

TRENT: Um, this may take a while. Hang on.

(He puts down the phone and moves out of the shot. Minutes pass as Daria looks first bored, then impatient, then sleepy. Her eyes are just dropping closed as Trent comes back.)

TRENT: Daria?

(Daria's eyes shoot open.)

DARIA: Uh, yeah, I'm here.

TRENT: Okay, I found a pen.

DARIA: You got paper?

(Trent looks around again. It's hopeless.)

TRENT: Uh, sure. Shoot.

DARIA: It's 516-3842.

(As she speaks, Trent scrawls the number on the wall over his bed.)

TRENT: Okay got it. I'll, uh, tell Janey to call you.

DARIA: 'Kay. (She yawns.) Guess I'll let you go. I'm kind of tired.

TRENT: Okay. Uh, Daria?

DARIA: Yeah?

TRENT: Come back soon, okay? Lawndale's pretty boring without you.

(Daria blushes again.)

DARIA: I, uh... (quickly) I miss you too Trent bye.

(She hangs up quickly.)

DARIA: I can't believe I did that.

(She gets up to go back to her room. Trent slowly hangs up the phone and smiles. Cut to the girls' room as Daria returns. This time, the lights are out and Quinn and Jennie are asleep. Daria takes off her glasses, lies down on a velvet couch and pulls a blanket over her. She tosses and turns a few minutes before falling into an uneasy sleep.)

(Cut to Sarah's room early the next afternoon, where Daria and Sarah are talking.)

SARAH: Tell me about your friends, Daria.

DARIA: Um, well, my best friend is named Jane. She likes art a lot, and she runs. Then sometimes we hang out with her older brother and his best friend. Other than those three, I don't really have many friends.

SARAH: Well, what's important is that you like the friends you do have. That's better than spending time with people you don't really like.

DARIA: Yeah. I guess you're right.

SARAH: Changing the subject, will you do me a favor?

DARIA: Sure. What?

SARAH: When the time comes, remind me to close my eyes, will you? I don't want to die with my eyes open. I've always been kinda worried that no one will be able to get 'em closed again.

(She laughs.)

DARIA: (smiles) I'll be sure to remind you.

(There is a thumping on the door. Daria turns to see Helen stick her head in the room.)

HELEN: Daria, I need to talk to you for a minute.

(Daria gets up and follows her mother out of the room.)

HELEN: Aunt Sarah's lawyer is in the living room--or is it the parlor? Or maybe they called it the drawing room. Anyway, he's here to see about her will. He said that he'd make a note of our requests, so maybe you'd better come in there and--

DARIA: (interrupting angrily) Is that all you care about? What you're going to get? I don't care what I get. You can have it all!

(She storms back into the bedroom and slams the door. Helen looks after her and glares.)

HELEN: What is the matter with that girl?

(Cut to inside the bedroom. Daria is sitting down again, glaring.)

SARAH: What was that all about?

DARIA: They're all staking claims on your stuff.

SARAH: (laughs) I wouldn't doubt it. (She smiles.) Daria, will you go get my lawyer and tell him I want to see him now? Before they start carting off my furniture.

DARIA: Okay.

(She gets up.)

SARAH: And don't let any of the family in here!

DARIA: (wryly) Don't worry.

(Cut to a couple hours later. The Morgendorffers are clotted anxiously around the bedroom door, when the lawyer emerges, looking sad.)

LAWYER: She wants you all to come in now. It's almost over.

(Cynthia bursts into melodramatic tears. Jennie follows her mother's suit, then Quinn, not to be outdone, also starts crying as the family gathers in the bedroom, followed by the lawyer.)

SARAH: (weakly) I'm sure my lawyer told you that my time is almost up. I can feel it, and I'm ready to let go now that everything is taken care of. After I'm gone, the contents of my will will be made known to you. I--

(She starts coughing again, but this time it's real. Her breath begins to come shorter, and the family backs away, looking somewhat scared. Daria glares and pushes through the people to sit down beside the bed. She takes Sarah's hand and looks down at her sadly. Sarah's eyes open again.)

SARAH: (softly) Daria... thank you. You're a... wonderful girl.

DARIA: (whispering) Thank you.

(Sarah smiles faintly and squeezes Daria's hand.)

DARIA: (whispering) Close your eyes.

(Sarah's smile grows a little, and she closes her eyes. The other Morgendorffers watch in hesitant silence a moment, then they realize that Sarah isn't moving at all.)

QUINN: Is she... dead?

(The young lawyer moves forward and feels for her pulse. He nods slowly. Quinn and Jennie both suddenly look a little scared and hurry out of the room. They are soon followed by the rest of the family. Daria slowly lets go of Sarah's hand and stands up. The lawyer puts his hand on her shoulder.)

LAWYER: I'm sorry.

DARIA: (surprised) Thanks.

(The lawyer leaves the room. Daria looks at Sarah another long moment, then follows him, shutting the door behind her.)

(Cut to the living room, where the rest of the family is gathered to hear the will. Daria enters and sits down on a chair in the back of the room as the lawyer moves to the front.)

LAWYER: The will is quite simple, actually. The house, and all possessions in it, are to be sold.

(There is a collective gasp from the Morgendorffers and harried murmuring. The lawyer holds up his hand for silence.)

LAWYER: The money made from the sale of the estate is to be donated to the Make-A-Wish Foundation, which, as some of you might know, grants the biggest wishes of children with terminal illnesses.

JENNIE: But what about us? We're children, and we're not dying!

LAWYER: (coldly) I haven't finished yet, miss. As I was saying, the estate will go to charity. There is, however, still the matter of Miss Morgendorffer's considerable fortune invested in the bank. Before I disclose the method of distribution for these funds, I will read a note that Miss Morgendorffer wrote this afternoon concerning her legacy.

(He unfolds a piece of paper and begins to read.)

LAWYER: "I wanted to leave my money to those who truly cared for me. That's the real reason I called all of you here, to judge who was most worthy of receiving my legacy. All of you have surpassed my expectations, and you have made my decision quite easy. Therefore, without further ado, here are the inheritants of my money."

(The lawyer pauses dramatically. Pan around the room showing the excited faces of the Morgendorffers... and Daria's deadpan expression. Cut back to the lawyer.)

LAWYER: "There are no inheritants!"

(There is another gasp from around the room.)

LAWYER: "There is only one inheritant. Every last penny is to go to Daria Morgendorffer."

ENTIRE MORGENDORFFER CLAN: Daria?!?

DARIA: (blankly) Me?

LAWYER: Please, ladies and gentlemen, let me finish! (He clears his throat.) "Daria was the only one out of all of you who showed me any consideration beyond what you thought was necessary to get all my money. Therefore, she's the only one who deserves it. I hope this will teach all of you a good lesson. I doubt it." (The lawyer quits reading and looks up.) The note ends there.

(Cut to shot of Daria as everyone stares at her.)

(Cut to later that night. Daria is sitting in a chair in an obscure room of the house, reading, when Jennie walks into the room.)

JENNIE: (beaming and sucking up) Daria, there's a phone call for you.

DARIA: Uh, thanks.

(She spots a phone on a table on the other side of the room and goes to pick up as Jennie tiptoes out of the room.)

DARIA: Jane?

TRENT: (phone VO) Uh, no, it's Trent.

DARIA: (surprised) Oh. Hey.

(Cut to split screen showing Trent in the Lanes' kitchen.)

TRENT: How's your great aunt?

DARIA: She, uh, died today.

TRENT: (embarrassed) Oh, uh, sorry.

DARIA: No, it's okay, you didn't... I mean, she... I... (Daria suddenly realizes that she's starting to cry.) Dammit!

TRENT: Uh, is this a bad time?

DARIA: No, I want... (Her voice is wavering and she tries to steady it.) I want to talk to somebody.

TRENT: (gently) Do you want me to get Janey?

DARIA: Actually, I, uh, want to talk to you.

TRENT: (looks pleased) Oh. Okay.

DARIA: (sniffs) Trent, I just feel really weird about all this. I mean, I only knew her since yesterday, but I feel... I feel like she understood me, and I understood her. And I feel horrible that she's gone, but I think I'm the only one. (She pauses and starts to wipe her eyes, then gives up. softly) I've never felt this way before. I've never been so damn sad. And to make things worse, she left all her money to me, so now everyone else is treating me just like they treated her--all nice and sappy, in the hopes that they'll get something out of me. I hate it here, but I don't know where I want to be.

(Her voice breaks, and she quits speaking. Jane comes into the room behind Trent, but he doesn't notice.)

TRENT: (softly) I wish I knew what to tell you, Daria, but I don't. (forcefully) Dammit, I wish I could help you. You shouldn't have to go through this.

(Jane looks surprised.)

DARIA: (sniffs again) Thanks, Trent. You're the first understanding person I've talked to all evening.

TRENT: (smiles a little) I try. Is the funeral tomorrow?

DARIA: Yeah. At one. (She sighs.) I wish I didn't have to go. It's going to be hard.

TRENT: If anyone can make it, Daria, you can.

DARIA: (blushes) Thanks. Again. Um, will you tell Jane I said hey? I think I just want to go to bed right now.

TRENT: Sure. Uh, if you want to talk to somebody tonight, go ahead and call. Mom and Dad are gone, so it's just me and Janey here.

DARIA: (smiles a little) Okay. See you, Trent.

TRENT: Yeah. I'll see you.

(Cut to shot of Trent only as he hangs up the phone.)

JANE: What happened?

(Trent jumps and turns around, looking embarrassed.)

TRENT: Damn, Janey, how long were you standing there?

JANE: Not long. I take it her great aunt died?

TRENT: Yeah. She's really broken up about it. (He looks at Jane determinedly.) But I have an idea.

(Cut to the exterior of Sarah's house late the next morning as a car pulls up. Zoom in to reveal that the car is Trent's. Trent and Jane get out and go to the door.)

TRENT: You ring it, Janey.

JANE: You ring it. This was your idea.

(Trent sighs and nervously pushes the doorbell. The maid opens the door and looks at the two disdainfully.)

MAID: Yes?

TRENT: Is, uh, Daria here?

(The maid immediately brightens.)

MAID: Ah, you are Daria's friends, then? Come in, come in.

(Trent and Jane look at each other, then follow the maid in. She leads them to the living room, then tells them to wait while she gets Daria.)

JANE: This place freaks me out. It's like living in the Addams Family's house.

DARIA: (offscreen VO) Dammit, Quinn, quit following me around!

JANE: I see that Daria's relationship with her sister is as healthy as ever.

(Daria comes into the room, still glaring, then stares in surprise when she sees Jane and Trent.)

DARIA: What are you doing here?

(She sits down in a chair near Jane's end of the sofa.)

TRENT: We thought you might want somebody here for the funeral. You know.

DARIA: I... well, thanks. But... you two drove three whole hours just for that?

JANE: (shrugs) We're your friends. 'Nuff said.

DARIA: (smiles weakly) Thanks. A lot. I really... (She breaks off.) I was just about to get ready for the funeral. Did you two bring any clothes?

(Jane and Trent look at each other.)

JANE: Oops.

(Daria stands up.)

DARIA: I've got some extra dresses. I guess you could wear one of them, Jane.

TRENT: What about me?

JANE: I don't think you and Daria are quite the same size, Trent.

(Daria and Trent glare at her.)

JANE: (innocently) What?

DARIA: There's some guy around here who might have a suit you could wear, Trent.

TRENT: What, he an uncle of yours or something?

DARIA: Uh, actually I'm not sure who he is. I just assumed that someone knew him. Anyway, his room is right down the hall. We can go ask.

(Trent and Jane get up and follow Daria to a nearby bedroom. Daria knocks on the door, which is answered by a skinny man of about thirty.)

DARIA: Travis, do you have an extra suit my friend can wear?

(Daria is quiet for a moment.)

DARIA: (looks up at the sky and smiles ever so slightly) No, I don't. In fact... I think she approves.

TRENT: Maybe it's the fate thing. You know. Like... what's meant to be will happen. (hesitantly) The only thing is... (He trails off.) Never mind.

DARIA: (looks at him) No, what?

TRENT: It's just... well, I've spent a lot of time waiting around for stuff, figuring that. Figuring that if it's meant to be, it'll happen. And so far, not much has happened.

DARIA: Well... maybe sometimes you wait for stuff to happen, and it does happen. Like Aunt Sarah just happened. And then sometimes you have to make it happen.

TRENT: (gets a deep-thought look) Whoa. I think you're right, Daria. There's, like, fate that just happens, and fate you can control. And... the biggest problem is knowing the difference.

DARIA: (a little nervously) So, did you enjoy your brush with fate this week?

TRENT: Yeah. I really did. It's the most excitement I've had in a while. What about you?

DARIA: (thoughtfully) Yeah... I enjoyed it too, in spite of all the stuff that happened.

(She looks at Trent. He's watching her encouragingly. Daria looks away and takes a deep breath as if she's about to say something, but she remains quiet.)

TRENT: What?

DARIA: (a little huntedly) I didn't say anything.

TRENT: (promptingly) But you started to.

DARIA: (slightly embarrassed) Oh, uh... I was just going to say... I, um, enjoyed getting to know you better.

TRENT: Oh. (matter of factly) Well, I liked spending time with you too.

(He doesn't seem particularly romantic about it, and Daria's face falls a little.)

DARIA: Well... I guess maybe I should be getting home before too much longer. I'm still sort of tired.

(Trent looks upset, but hides it quickly.)

TRENT: C'mon, stick around at least a little longer. It's not that late.

DARIA: Um... all right.

(There is a long pause, then...)

TRENT: Daria--

DARIA: (at the same time) Trent--

(They look at each other and laugh. Almost as suddenly they stop laughing and just look at each other.)

DARIA: (quietly) What were you going to say?

(Trent hesistates, then very slowly reaches out and touches her cheek. Daria freezes as still as a rabbit caught in headlights, her eyes wide.)

TRENT: I was just going to say what I started to say in the giant golf ball.

DARIA: (still paralyzed) Wh-what?

(Trent hesistates only a moment more.)

TRENT: (whispering very softly) This.

(He slowly leans over and gently kisses her lips. Daria doesn't react at all; she just stays frozen. Trent starts to pull away, looking disappointed, when Daria suddenly leans forward and kisses him back. The kiss is not very long but infinitely gentle. Then she pulls back.)

DARIA: (mumbling) I, uh... really had better get home. My parents--

TRENT: (gently) Okay. (He smiles at her.) Good night, Daria.

DARIA: (looks up shyly and smiles back just a little) 'Night, Trent.

(She gets up quickly and hurries off, looking a little overwhelmed. Trent watches her go, still smiling a little, then he leans back and looks up at the stars. Pan past him to show Jane and Amy just inside the door to McGrundy's, unnoticed. Jane looks positively shocked; Amy is only smiling softly.)

JANE: I... I can't believe it. They did it. They finally did it!

AMY: (wryly) And if they knew you'd seen them, it would have been the last thing you witnessed.

JANE: I... oh man. (She leans against the door frame as a slow grin spreads over her face.) Finally. All my hard work paid off. (slightly annoyed) But why did she just get up and leave? I mean... geez!

AMY: (puts her hand on Jane's shoulder, smiling knowingly) Give her time, Jane. It's a big step for her. And Trent too, I think.

(Jane looks at her brother for a long minute.)

JANE: You know... I think you're right.

AMY: (gently) Come on, let's go back in. (smirking) I gave in and promised Gregory a dance.

JANE: (grins back) They're going to make a goth of you yet.

(The two turn and go back inside. Slow fade to the interior of McGrundy's as the Harpies begin to play "Closing Time" by Semisonic. Zoom in on Monique as she begins to sing an almost straight cover of the song. Montage of scenes begins as she sings...)

MONIQUE: (singing) Closing time, time for you to go out, go out into the world.

(Pan out from her to show the whole band: her, Veronica, and Andrea. Linger on them a moment, the pan around the room.)

MONIQUE: (singing) Closing time, turn all of the lights up over every boy and every girl.

(Amy and Gregory are dancing; she is talking while he actually laughs. Continue pan. Jane is watching the band play; Stacy is dancing with a goth (and looking a little bemused). Jesse and Erik are talking; Erik finally seems happy.)

MONIQUE: (singing VO) Closing time, one last call for alcohol, so finish your whiskey or beer. Closing time, you don't have to go home but you can't stay here...

(Zoom out of McGrundy's door, past where Trent is still sitting on the curb. He is staring up at the sky, looking introspective.)

MONIQUE: (singing VO) I know who I want to take me home, I know who I want to take me home, I know who I want to take me home, take me home...

(Scene fades to the interior of Chez Pierre, but Monique's song continues as a voice-over.)

MONIQUE: (singing VO) Closing time, time for you to go back to the places you will be from.

(Quinn and Jamie, both dressed elegantly, are seated at romantic table, complete with candles and flowers. They are staring into each others' eyes and smiling.)

MONIQUE: (singing VO) Closing time, this room won't be open 'til your brothers or your sisters come.

(Scene fades to a movie theater. Joey, Sandi, Tiffany, and Jeffy are sitting in a row, watching a romantic comedy. The girls are sipping diet drinks and seem content; the boys look miserable.)

(Finally, fade to Daria walking home alone. She is looking up at the sky.)

MONIQUE: (singing VO) So gather up your jackets, and move it to the exits... I hope you have found a friend.

(Close-up on Daria's face as she stops walking.)

MONIQUE: (singing VO) Closing time, every new beginning comes from some other beginning's end...

(The singing and music stop, leaving complete silence.)

(Pan past Daria's profile to the sky. It is nearly completely black, spangled with a million tiny stars. Cut back to close-up of Daria.)

DARIA: (quietly) Fate? Is it real? (There is the very slightest hint of tears in her eyes.) Aunt Sarah, I'm scared.

(Cut back to the sky. It is still for a moment, then a meteor slices across the darkness, leaving a trail of light behind. Cut back to Daria as a single tear leaves a matching trail on her cheek. Very slowly, she smiles.)

(Pull back as Daria begins walking again. Suddenly fade in the original version of "Closing Time" as pull back continues.)

...I know who I want to take me home...
...I know who I want to take me home...
...I know who I want to take me home...
...Take me home...

(Slowly fade to black.)
