Fan Fiction
Episode Novelizations

In the grand tradition of movie novelizations, the following stories are prose novelizations of the scripts of various Daria episodes. In addition to almost all of the original dialogue, there are various changes and additions (many of them subtle, some of them significant) designed to enhance the original story. If you would like to experience classic Daria in a whole new way, give these novelizations a try. (Additional episodes will be added over time, eventually covering the entire series run of Daria.)


Season 1
#101 - Esteemsters
Screenplay by Glenn Eichler
Novelization by Martin J. Pollard

When Daria begins attending classes at Lawndale High, she is identified as having low self-esteem and is put in a special class. There, she meets fellow misfit Jane Lane, and together they scheme to get out of the class.
#102 - The Invitation
Screenplay by Anne D. Bernstein
Novelization by Martin J. Pollard

Daria helps Brittany in class, which gets her invited to Brittany's big party. Problem is, Quinn's invited, too, and she's worried that Daria will ruin her standing with the popular crowd.
#111 - Road Worrier
Screenplay by Anne D. Bernstein
Novelization by Brielle Potter

Daria and Jane head to the Alternapalooza festival with Trent and Mystik Spiral bandmate Jesse. On the trip, Daria's day goes steadily downhill as every imaginable humiliation happens to her... and all in front of Trent.
#113 - The Misery Chick
Screenplay by Glenn Eichler
Novelization by Adam Spradlin

Football hero Tommy Sherman returns to Lawndale High to be honored by the school, but when the obnoxious alumnus is killed in a tragic accident, it's "misery chick" Daria that others turn to for help in coping with his death.

Season 2
#203 - Quinn the Brain
Screenplay by Rachel Lipman
Novelization by Martin J. Pollard

When Quinn unexpectedly receives a high grade on an essay, everyone starts thinking of her as a "brain," much to Daria's chagrin.
#209 - Ill
Screenplay by Peter Gaffney
Novelization by Martin J. Pollard

Daria comes down with a mysterious skin rash while attending a Mystik Sprial gig, and no one can find the cause... or a cure.
#212 - Pierce Me
Screenplay by Neena Beber
Novelization by Martin J. Pollard

Trent asks Daria to help him pick out a birthday gift for Jane, but ends up talking Daria into getting her navel pierced, while Quinn convinces Helen to participate in a mother/daughter fashion show at school.
#213 - Write Where it Hurts
Screenplay by Glenn Eichler
Novelization by Martin J. Pollard

Daria has to write a short story for English class, using people she knows as fictional characters. There's just one problem: she can't come up with anything.

Season 3
#301 - Through A Lens Darkly
Screenplay by Glenn Eichler
Novelization by Wilson Perez

A near-disastrous driving lesson prompts Daria to get contact lenses. Her reluctance is soon forgotten in the face of the reaction she receives from others... and her own reaction when she finds she can't wear them anymore.
#308 - Lane Miserables
Screenplay by Anne D. Bernstein
Novelization by Martin J. Pollard

It's madness and mayhem as all the prodigal Lanes return to the nest at once, forcing Jane and Trent to seek refuge with the Morgendorffers.
#311 - The Lawndale File
Screenplay by Peter Elwell
Novelization by Bacner

Strange goings-on at school and at home kicks Daria's and Jane's paranoid levels sky high.

Season 4
#402 - Antisocial Climbers
Screenplay by Jill Cargerman
Novelization by Bacner

An assignment-inspired camping trip in the woods turns into a disaster for Daria, Jane, and other Lawndale High students and teachers.
#413 - Dye! Dye! My Darling
Screenplay by Glenn Eichler
Novelization by Mike Yamiolkoski

A request for help from Jane becomes a personal nightmare for Daria, who has to deal not only with Jane's jealousy and possessivness over Tom, but with her own confused feelings for him as well.

Season 5
#506 - Lucky Strike
Screenplay by Peter Elwell
Novelization by Marielle Scheid

When Lawndale High's teachers go out on strike, Daria finds herself roped into becoming a substitute. The fact that she's teaching Quinn's English class could be a bad thing, or something very, very good.
#513 - Boxing Daria
Screenplay by Glenn Eichler
Novelization by Adam Spradlin

Daria confronts powerful repressed childhood memories triggered by a refrigerator box... and the memories aren't good ones.

Other
Sealed With a Kick
Screenplay by Sam Johnson & Chris Marcil
Novelization by CF

Daria Morgendorffer, a cynical, brainy newcomer to Lawndale High School, exacts revenge on a football player who insults her. (This was the animatic pilot episode that never aired on MTV. It was included on the first Daria video as a bonus.)
Daria's Inferno
Written by Peter Elwell
Novelization by CF

Daria falls asleep in Mr O'Neill's English class during a lesson on Dante's Inferno and dreams of her own hell: Lawndale! Based on the video game from Simon & Schuster Interactive.
