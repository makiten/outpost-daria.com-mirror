Finally, a note about a slight change to the site. Previously, links to external pages (i.e. outside of outpost-daria.com) would open in the same browser window. I've changed things so that they now open in a separate window. This would normally be something of a pain, but most modern browsers (including Mozilla Firefox and Internet Explorer 7) now support tabbed browsing, and since they can be configured to open new windows in new tabs, this pain is lessened considerably.

(If you aren't currently using a browser that supports tabbed browsing, I highly suggest one as a Christmas gift to yourself. Based on my experience, once you've experienced tabbed browsing, you'll never go back.)

May 2006
May 31
 		Finally, an update! My apologies for the long delay; part of the blame can be laid on a catastrophic system failure back in early February, one where I lost absolutely everything stored on my system (and I do mean everything: my system's hard drives literally went up in a puff of smoke!).

Fortunately, I had copies of some stuff (including a complete copy of the website) on CD-R's and on my work computer, and I was able to easily replace the other hardware that was damaged. It's been a long road to this point to rebuild what I had, however, and sadly, there are still a lot of files and information that I'll never recover, including stuff dating back nearly 15 years. :-(

Needless to say, I'm now a big believer in backups of ALL kinds, including media and power! Nightly backups to a USB hard drive, CD-RW images of my DOS and Windows partitions whenever anything changes, and a UPS with power surge protection are going to ensure that I never have to go through this again. <fingers crossed>

As for the remaining reasons for such a long delay, chalk it up to plenty of real life distractions (both personal and professional). However, I now seem to be nearing the end of the tunnel, and the light does not appear to be an oncoming train. :-)

(Final note: there's a lot more in the backlog, including more artwork and a few transcripts courtesy of Richard Lobinske, but those'll have to wait for the next update, as this one's far too long overdue.)

Enjoy the long-awaited update!
